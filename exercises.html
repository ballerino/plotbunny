Writing Exercises

Doing writing exercises is a common practice to expand your imagination and psu hthe boundaries of your writing. The writing exercises on this page are created to help you think about, examine, and transform your writing habits. Give them a try!
Suppose the human race creates an android that looks and acts exactly like a human. Do you think people will welcome the androids or become repulsed by the idea of them? What are some human elements do you think androids will not be able to do? Is the idea of making a machine act like a human being flawed?
Describe the setting of your next work only by senses other than sight. What does it feel like? What does it smell like? What noises are heard?
Write about a character (or yourself!) waking up in a foreign place, and not knowing how they got there! How will your character react? How will they solve their predicament? What follows after?
What are different ways that people can show intimacy? Think about the physical space between two intimate people. How do they react to each other when they are far apart? How different is it when they are close in proximity to each other? Are they in love and if not, why are they not in love? Write a story or a poem about two people complicatedly in love.
What triggers anger for your antagonist? Does this also trigger anger in you? Do you find yourself similar or dissimilar to your antagonist? Are there values in your antagonist that you like or have yourself?
Do you ever wish you are older or younger than you are right now? What are the benefits of either one? If you were trapped in the body of an older person, how different would you act? How about in a younger body?
Think about the quote "money is the root of all evil". Do you or do your characters agree with this quote? Is money power? If so, do you think having power is the root of all evil? What is evil?
