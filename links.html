Helpful Links

Writing is about more than your pen and paper. Due to creative writing software and technology, writing articles, web generators, writing and publishing communities, and more, writing is more interactive than before! Our helpful links pages will be updated weekly with new material. Remember, writing advice is subjective. Everyone writes differently. The links we provide are to be taken more as suggestions rather than professional reviews. (We are not paid to advertise any of the software or websites!)

Do you have a recommendation you want to share? Send us an email on our contact page.

Writing Software

  1. Ommwriter
    Ommwriter provides a writing environment on PC, MAC, or iPad. When you turn on the program, it automatically blocks out everything else you have on your desktop - sounds, pictures, everything - so that you can write without the temptation of distraction. Ommwriter provides beautifully subtle backgrounds and soothing music to get you into a peaceful writing mood. They have a free version, or a donated version to choose from.
  2. Liquid Story Binder XE
    This software is a portable text editor created by Black Obelisk software. It is designed more towards fiction writing (novels, screen writing), rather than poetry. You can organize your documents, your story by chapters, and also archive media, such as music and images, that inspire you. Free download.
  3. Scrivener
    Scrivener is a popular writing software that is recommended by many writing communities. You can use this software to plan stories, write essays, or write notes/reminders. There is a large emphasis on structure and project research when you are using this software. There is a free trial and a paid version on the website.
  4. yWriter5
    yWriter is a free novel writing software for Windows computer that helps you keep track of the chapters and scenes in your story. It is easy to write in different scenes when a new idea arises with this software. yWriter also stores past files in case you lose your files!

Generators

  1. Seventh Sanctum
    This is a web page of free generators for any of your writing or role-playing needs! They have traditional generators such as name and settings generators, but they also have wacky and unique ones too! If you're looking for a random name for technology, a ship, an organization, or anything, Seventh Sanctum is a great place to browse.
  2. They Fight Crime
    They Fight Crime is a fun generator that generates two random characters that become crime fighter comrades. You can use this generator to create a writing exercise for yourself!
  3. Springhole.Net - Random Generators
    Springhole.net is a website that offers writing and role-playing advice. This link is to their random generators web page. It includes but is not limited to a world-building related generator, a name generator, and a origin story generator.

Helpful Websites

  1. Fifteen minutes of Fiction
    Every week, the site posts up a new prompt and you can respond to it with a short piece of writing! It's great for people who don't have a lot of time tine to write!
  2. Grammar Book
    This is the site to use on your editing home stretch! I believe most people don't have a problem with the actual story idea and getting through the writing, but with GRAMMAR. Check out how to use hyphens and semicolons.

Databases

  1. Duotrope's Digest
    This site offers a huge database of current fiction and poetry.They have more than 5,000 works listed, and they update often (a year ago they had 4,000)! You can search for certain genres, styles, themes. It's nice to see what's out there!
  2. Great Opening sentences
    An interesting website that provides a list of famous and favorite opening sentences.
  3. Folktexts
    Folktexts is a webpage edited by D. L. Ashliman that contains links to several electronic versions of fairy tales and folklores across many different cultures. You can find stories from Germany, France, India, and more.

Writing Communities

  1. NanoWriMo
    NanoWriMo - or National Novel Writing Month, is a nonprofit that organizes writing events hosted world wide, thanks to the internet. They are well known for hosting events for young writers, and the NaNoWriMo where participants try to write a novel of 50,000 words in one month.
  2. Wattpad
    Wattpad is one of the biggest online communities of writers and readers. It is a social platform where people can show their work or discover someone else's work. You can upload short stories, chaptered work, poetry, blog posts, and fan fiction.
